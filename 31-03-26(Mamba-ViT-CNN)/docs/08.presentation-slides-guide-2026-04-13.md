## SLIDE 6: Copy-Paste — Why It Failed (Analysis)

**Title:** Copy-Paste — Why It's a Good Negative Result

**Content (bullet points):**

**Why it failed:**
1. `copy_paste=0.5` was too aggressive — flooded 50% of training images with pasted small objects
2. Combined with `mixup=0.15` + `flipud=0.5` + `degrees=10.0` — too many augmentations stacked simultaneously
3. Model got "distracted" — learned to over-detect tiny objects at the expense of medium ones
4. C2A already has ~35 objects/image at extreme density — artificially adding more created unrealistic crowding
5. Val medium recall dropped even worse: 0.821 -> 0.659 (-16.2%)

**Why it's a valid negative result for the paper:**
1. Copy-paste is widely recommended for small object detection (COCO, VisDrone literature)
2. Shows that C2A's extreme density + small object ratio (47% < 10px) makes copy-paste counterproductive
3. The model was already well-tuned — the no-aug training was sufficient
4. **Decision: Inference-time enhancement (SAHI/TTA) is the right approach, not training-time augmentation**

## SLIDE 9: SAHI — What It Does

**Title:** SAHI: Slicing Aided Hyper Inference

**Diagram/Visual explanation:**
- Show an aerial image being sliced into overlapping tiles
- Each tile processed independently at model's native 640x640 resolution
- Detections merged using postprocessing
- Full-image prediction also runs to catch medium/large objects

**Key improvements in our SAHI configuration:**
| Setting | Naive/Default | Our Optimized Config |
|---------|--------------|---------------------|
| Postprocess | NMS | **GREEDYNMM** (merges instead of discarding) |
| Match metric | IoU | **IOS** (handles size-varying duplicates) |
| Full-image pred | Off | **ON** (preserves medium/large recall) |
| Slice sizes | Single size | **4-config sweep: 256, 320, 512, 640** |
| Confidence | 0.25 | **0.15-0.20** (low-conf tiny objects pass) |

**Why these matter for C2A:**
- C2A has 20-40 objects per image — GREEDYNMM prevents duplicate suppression
- IOS handles adjacent-slice overlapping detections of different sizes
- `perform_standard_pred=True` is critical — previous attempt without it lost -13.3% medium recall

## SLIDE 13: SAHI + TTA Combined — Non-Result

**Title:** SAHI + TTA Combined

**Source:** `SAHI+TTA/benchmark_reports/grand_summary_noaug.json`

**Table:**

| Config | P | R | F1 | F2 | VT Recall | Latency |
|--------|---|---|----|----|-----------|---------| 
| SAHI-256 alone | 0.7899 | 0.8720 | 0.8289 | 0.8542 | 0.8292 | 534ms |
| SAHI-256 + TTA | 0.7899 | 0.8720 | 0.8289 | 0.8542 | 0.8292 | 530ms |

**Finding:** Identical metrics across ALL measures. TTA adds NOTHING when combined with SAHI.

**Why (technical explanation):**
- SAHI slices images to 256x256 — already smaller than 640px training resolution
- TTA's multi-scale passes at slice level (e.g., 0.67x of 256 = 172px) see features far below training distribution
- TTA's value comes from processing LARGER than training resolution — useless on small slices
- The monkey-patch wraps `model.__call__` with `augment=True`, but at 256px slice level it has no benefit

**Decision:** Report SAHI and TTA as independent, complementary techniques. Don't combine them.

## SLIDE 20: What Didn't Work (Discussion Points)

**Title:** What Didn't Work — Discussion

**Bullet points with brief analysis:**

1. **Copy-Paste Augmentation:** -1.67% mAP50-95, -13.25% medium recall. Too aggressive for C2A's already dense annotations (~35 obj/image). Copy-paste works on sparse datasets (COCO: ~7 obj/image) but fails on dense ones.

2. **TTA at 1920:** mAP50 degraded to 0.866 (vs 0.890 at 1280). Model trained at 640px can't extrapolate to 3x resolution. The 1.0x scale at 1920px sees feature distributions never encountered in training.

3. **SAHI + TTA Combined:** Identical to SAHI alone. TTA is redundant when slices (256px) are already smaller than training resolution (640px). Multi-scale at slice level adds no information.

4. **AtrousMamba (previous week):** -10% mAP50-95 (0.554 vs 0.654). Dilated SSM scanning added +20.5% params, +41.1% GFLOPs, 2.6x slower — all for worse accuracy. The 3 parallel dilated branches overcomplicated the model.
