## 4. The Uncomfortable Truth About MDPI and Scientific Reports

You should know this before choosing:

1. **Both are legitimate, Q1-indexed, peer-reviewed journals.** They appear in Web of Science and Scopus.
2. **Both charge APCs** (~$2400-$2700 USD) — this is NOT the same as predatory publishing, but it IS a pay-to-publish open access model.
3. **Some academics dismiss MDPI** as "low quality" because of fast review times and high acceptance volume. This criticism is partially fair but mostly snobbish. Drones and Remote Sensing are genuinely solid venues.
4. **Scientific Reports** has a similar reputation issue. It's Nature-published but has extremely high volume.
5. **For an undergrad thesis in Bangladesh, publishing in Drones or Scientific Reports is a solid achievement.** Don't let MDPI snobbery stop you if your advisor is supportive.
6. **Check with your institution:** Does your university recognize MDPI/Scientific Reports for graduation requirements? Some Asian universities do NOT count MDPI papers for certain purposes.

## 8. What Q1/Q2 Actually Means

Just so you know:
- **Q1** = top 25% of journals in that specific subject category (not all journals!)
- A Q1 journal in "Remote Sensing" is not the same as a Q1 journal in "Artificial Intelligence"
- Drones is Q1 in Remote Sensing but would be much lower if categorized under AI
- IEEE Access shifts between Q1 and Q2 depending on the year and category
- **Q1 ≠ top journal.** TPAMI is Q1. Drones is also Q1. Huge quality gap between them.

For a Bangladeshi undergraduate CSE student, **a Q1 paper in Drones, Scientific Reports, or Remote Sensing is an excellent outcome.** Don't let anyone tell you otherwise.
