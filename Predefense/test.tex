{
\begin{flashleft}
    
Off-chain transactions aim to address the limited scalability of on-chain systems and enable blockchain-based payments over unreliable networks. However, existing solutions often struggle to balance security and flexibility in their design. This paper proposes a novel blockchain-based offline payment protocol that addresses this challenge. We hypothesize that two offline parties with loosely synchronized clocks and communication channels of known, bounded latency can securely conduct off-chain transactions while preserving flexibility. Our protocol integrates on-chain smart contracts with offline wallet interactions to ensure resilience against intermittent blockchain connectivity. It also leverages platform-agnostic Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs), such as Intel Software Guard Extensions (SGX), to provide secure and flexible computations. We empirically evaluate the protocol's performance, demonstrating its high efficiency and advanced security in comparison to existing state-of-the-art solutions. Through rigorous security analysis and comparison against real-world attack scenarios, we prove the robustness of our scheme within a universally composable framework, making it a secure and user-friendly solution for offline blockchain payments.
\end{flashleft}
