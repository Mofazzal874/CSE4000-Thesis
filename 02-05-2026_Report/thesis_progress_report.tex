\section{Introduction}
% =============================================================
Search-and-Rescue (SAR) operations after natural disasters rely on
UAVs to scan large affected regions for human survivors. Detecting
humans in such imagery is hard: targets are extremely small, often
occluded, in unusual poses, and embedded in post-disaster clutter.
The \textbf{C2A dataset}~\cite{nihal2024c2a} is the largest public
benchmark for this task: 10{,}215 images, $>$360{,}000 person
instances, of which \textbf{47\% are smaller than 10\,pixels} and 99\%
are below 50\,pixels -- substantially harder than VisDrone or SARD.

Earlier stages of this thesis benchmarked YOLOv9/YOLOv11 on C2A and
explored two SSM integrations: a \textit{Mamba+CBAM+P2} variant that
became our best model, and an \textit{AtrousMamba} variant that lost
$10$ points of mAP$_{50\text{-}95}$ and was abandoned. Mamba+CBAM+P2
gave competitive overall mAP but its very-tiny recall was the weakest
cell of every ablation. This report covers the April 2026 work
targeting that weakness. Fig.~\ref{fig:dataset} shows representative
C2A samples.

\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.92\linewidth]{labels.jpg}
\caption{Representative C2A annotations: survivors appear as sub-10\,px
instances against post-disaster clutter.}
\label{fig:dataset}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{(\arabic*)}]
\item Full evaluation of \textbf{Mamba+CBAM+P2 YOLO11m} (bidirectional
local-window SSM in the neck plus a P2 head) trained on C2A.
\item A controlled \textbf{Copy-Paste} ablation reported as a negative
result, motivating the shift to inference-time enhancement.
\item A systematic sweep of \textbf{SAHI} (4 slice configs, GREEDYNMM)
and \textbf{TTA} (3 resolutions), with an analysis of why their
combination gives no additional gain.
\item A final comparison against Nihal~et~al.~\cite{nihal2024c2a}: our
$19.6$\,M-parameter model with TTA@1280 matches or exceeds YOLOv9-e
($57.3$\,M) on C2A.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Methodology}
% =============================================================
\subsection{Dataset and Pre-processing}
We use the public C2A split~\cite{nihal2024c2a} (8{,}172 train,
1{,}021 val, 2{,}043 test images; $\approx$360k person instances over
five poses merged into one \texttt{person} class). Images are
letterboxed to $640\!\times\!640$ for training; for evaluation we use
$832$/$1280$/$1920$ for TTA and tile sizes $\{256,320,512,640\}$ for
SAHI.

Boxes are bucketed by longer side into \textit{very-tiny} ($<$8\,px),
\textit{tiny} (8--16), \textit{small} (16--32) and \textit{medium}
(32--96), yielding 25{,}072 / 20{,}520 / 26{,}614 / 317 test instances
(34.6\% very-tiny, 0.4\% medium, no objects $>$96\,px).

\subsection{Architecture: Mamba+CBAM+P2 YOLO11m}
Our detector extends \texttt{ultralytics} YOLO11m with three changes:
(i)~a CBAM block at the end of the backbone, (ii)~an additional P2
detection head, and (iii)~a bidirectional local-window Mamba SSM
bottleneck in every neck \texttt{C3k2} block. The full topology is
defined in \texttt{yolov11m\_cbam\_p2head.yaml}.

\paragraph{CBAM block.}
CBAM~\cite{woo2018cbam} re-weights features along the channel then
spatial axes. For
$\mathbf{F}\!\in\!\mathbb{R}^{C\times H\times W}$,
\begin{equation}\small
\mathbf{F}'=\mathbf{M}_c(\mathbf{F})\!\otimes\!\mathbf{F},\quad
\mathbf{F}''=\mathbf{M}_s(\mathbf{F}')\!\otimes\!\mathbf{F}',
\end{equation}
where
$\mathbf{M}_c=\sigma(\mathrm{MLP}(\mathrm{AvgPool}\,\mathbf{F})+\mathrm{MLP}(\mathrm{MaxPool}\,\mathbf{F}))$
uses a shared $1\!\times\!1$ MLP ($r{=}16$) and
$\mathbf{M}_s=\sigma(\mathrm{conv}_{7\times7}([\mathrm{AvgPool}_c\mathbf{F}';\mathrm{MaxPool}_c\mathbf{F}']))$.
We insert it after \texttt{SPPF} in place of the default
\texttt{C2PSA}.

\paragraph{P2 detection head.}
Default YOLO11m detects on \{P3,P4,P5\} (strides 8/16/32). We add a
stride-4 P2 head by upsampling the neck once more and concatenating
with the stride-4 backbone activation, giving four scales
$160^2/80^2/40^2/20^2$ at $640$\,px input. The motivation is
geometric: a 6\,px person is only $0.75$ feature pixels at P3 but
$1.5$ at P2, crossing the threshold below which YOLO loses the object
outright. Cost is $+28.2\%$ GFLOPs.

\paragraph{Mamba bottleneck (C3K2Mamba).}
A linear State-Space Model~\cite{gu2024mamba}
$h'\!=\!Ah+Bu,\,y\!=\!Ch+Du$ is discretised by Zero-Order-Hold into
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ssm}\small
\bar{A}_t\!=\!\exp(\Delta_t A),\;
x_t\!=\!\bar{A}_t x_{t-1}\!+\!\Delta_t B_t u_t,\;
y_t\!=\!C_t^{\!\top}x_t\!+\!D\!\odot\!u_t,
\end{equation}
with $(\Delta_t,B_t,C_t)$ made input-dependent
($=\mathrm{Linear}(u_t)$). This \textbf{selective scan} costs $O(DN)$
per step with $N\!=\!4$, giving $O(L)$ complexity in sequence length
$L$ -- vs.\ attention's $O(L^2)$.

We adapt it to 2-D as a \textbf{LocalWindowSSM}: partition the feature
map into $w\!\times\!w$ windows, run forward and backward scans (with
separate weights) along each flattened window, and merge via
$(y_{\text{fwd}}\!+\!y_{\text{bwd}})\!\cdot\!\mathrm{SiLU}(z)$ before
the output projection and reverse-windowing. Window size is adaptive
($w\!\in\!\{4,6,8\}$ for channels
$\{{\geq}512,256,128\}$) so per-window length stays $\le 64$ and the
scan is tractable in pure PyTorch. $A\!=\!-\exp(A_{\log})$ keeps the
continuous SSM stable; SSM ops run in FP32 under AMP. Local windows
preserve spatial locality (the failure mode of AtrousMamba was
exactly the loss of locality from dilated raster scans); the
bidirectional pair compensates for the unidirectional recurrence.

The C3K2Mamba block follows the C2f split--bottleneck--concat layout
with each bottleneck applying
$\texttt{Conv}_{3\!\times\!3}\!\to\!\texttt{LocalWindowSSM}\!\to\!\texttt{Conv}_{3\!\times\!3}$
plus a residual when channel counts match.

\subsection{Training Configuration}
Training runs on Kaggle dual T4 GPUs (DDP, BF16), 120 epochs, SGD
(momentum 0.937, lr 0.01 cosine, wd $5\!\times\!10^{-4}$), batch~8 per
GPU with grad-accum~2 (effective batch~16), default YOLO mosaic+HSV
augmentations, mosaic closed for the last 10 epochs. Wall-clock
$\approx\!8$\,h. The model has \textbf{19.59\,M parameters / 43.7
GFLOPs} at $640\!\times\!640$, $\approx\!3\times$ smaller than
YOLOv9-e.

\subsection{Copy-Paste Augmentation Variant}
To push very-tiny recall higher we retrained the same architecture
with aggressive small-object augmentation:
\texttt{copy\_paste}=0.5, \texttt{mixup}=0.15, \texttt{flipud}=0.5,
\texttt{degrees}=10$^{\circ}$, all else unchanged. The previous no-aug
\texttt{best.pt} serves as the controlled comparison.

\subsection{Inference-Time Enhancement}
Two complementary techniques are evaluated on the no-aug
\texttt{best.pt}; neither requires retraining.

\textbf{(a) SAHI~\cite{akyon2022sahi}} tiles each image into
overlapping slices, each resized to the model's $640^2$ input
(effectively up-sampling sub-10\,px humans by the slice/640 ratio),
detects per slice, and merges back. Our configuration differs from
the defaults in three ways: \textbf{GREEDYNMM} (merges overlapping
neighbour-tile detections instead of suppressing them — correct for
dense scenes), \textbf{IOS} match metric (normalises by the smaller
box, robust to size-varying duplicates from adjacent tiles), and
\textbf{\texttt{perform\_standard\_pred=True}} (also runs a full-image
pass; without it medium objects spanning tile borders are lost — an
earlier prototype lost $-13.3$ pp medium recall). We sweep slice sizes
$\{256,320,512,640\}$ with overlap $\{0.30,0.25,0.25,0.30\}$ and
slice-level confidence $\{0.15,0.15,0.20,0.20\}$.

\textbf{(b) TTA} -- Ultralytics \texttt{val(augment=True)} runs three
forward passes per image at scales $\{1.0,0.83,0.67\}$ with a
horizontal flip on the second pass and merges via NMS. We sweep input
resolution $\{832,1280,1920\}$.

\subsection{Evaluation Protocol}
Baseline and TTA rows use Ultralytics \texttt{val()} for official
mAP$_{50}$ and mAP$_{50\text{-}95}$. SAHI rows use custom per-image
TP/FP/FN matching at IoU=0.5 (the standard SAHI protocol; mAP is not
defined here because SAHI bypasses the Ultralytics validator). We
additionally report $P$, $R$, $F_1$, the recall-weighted
$F_2\!=\!5PR/(4P+R)$, per-size recall, and 15-bin ECE. $F_2$ is
emphasised because in SAR a missed victim costs more than a false
alarm.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
C2A already carries $\sim$35 annotations per image; pasting more small
humans into 50\% of training images creates an unrealistic crowding
regime that biases the model toward tiny objects at the expense of the
rare medium category. Copy-paste is widely recommended for small
objects (COCO has $\sim$7 instances per image), but the
recommendation \emph{flips} when the dataset is already dense. We
therefore retain the no-aug weights as base and turn to inference-time
techniques.

\subsection{Test-Time Augmentation Sweep}
Table~\ref{tab:tta} sweeps the inference resolution. TTA@1280 yields
the best mAP$_{50}$ ($0.890$) and the largest mAP$_{50\text{-}95}$
gain in this work ($+5.4$\,pp). At $1920$ the model is asked to
extrapolate to $3\times$ its training resolution and mAP$_{50}$
regresses below the 832\,px result.

\paragraph{Why TTA helps mAP$_{50\text{-}95}$ more than mAP$_{50}$.}
mAP$_{50\text{-}95}$ averages across IoU $\{0.50,\ldots,0.95\}$ and so
rewards precise localisation; TTA's multi-scale fusion produces
tighter boxes through cross-scale agreement, which is why the strict-
IoU gain ($+5.4$\,pp) far exceeds the IoU=0.5 gain ($+1.6$\,pp).

\paragraph{SAHI+TTA combined: a non-result.}
Injecting \texttt{augment=True} per slice produces metrics
indistinguishable from SAHI-256 alone. At 256\,px slice size, TTA's
$\{0.67,1.0,1.5\}\!\times$ scales operate at $\{172,256,384\}$\,px --
all below the $640$\,px training resolution -- so TTA contributes no
information. The two techniques are reported as alternatives, not a
stack.

\subsection{Summary and What Did Not Work}
\textbf{Best numbers.} \emph{Overall accuracy} -- Mamba+CBAM+P2 +
TTA@1280: mAP$_{50}\!=\!0.890$, mAP$_{50\text{-}95}\!=\!0.698$,
beating YOLOv9-e by $+1.0$\,pp at $\approx\!3\times$ fewer parameters.
\emph{Very-tiny recall} -- Mamba+CBAM+P2 + SAHI-256: VT~$=0.829$
($+6.24$\,pp), medium bucket preserved at $0.896$.

\textbf{What did not work.} \emph{Copy-Paste}
($-1.7$\,mAP$_{50\text{-}95}$, $-13.3$\,medium-recall);
\emph{TTA@1920} (degrades vs.\ 1280 because $3\times$ training
resolution is too far); \emph{SAHI+TTA combined} (no gain — slices
already smaller than training resolution); \emph{AtrousMamba}
($-10$\,mAP$_{50\text{-}95}$ — dilated SSM scanning broke spatial
locality). Eliminating these delineates what works on extreme-density
small-object datasets.

\textbf{Next steps.} (i) HERIDAL cross-dataset evaluation, (ii) SAHI
confidence tuning to recover SAHI-256 precision, (iii) journal
manuscript for \emph{Drones} (MDPI, Q1).
